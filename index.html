What is the course about?

Design and the study of algorithms goes beyond being a single field of computer science. It forms the foundation of computer science, and, in all honesty, may be considered applicable to much of science, industry, and technology. This course focuses on recognizing fascinating patterns found in nature, since many natural phenomena can motivate computational algorithms and conversely. It supports the growth of analytical reasoning, which can assist in diverse problem solving approaches and techniques.

Data Structures and Algorithms Studied

Studying data structures feels like traveling within an intriguing family. From the seamless flow of memory locations in arrays and strings to the connected behavior of web pages reflected in linked lists, every structure narrates a tale. Stacks and queues suggest familiar scenes of biscuit stacks and waiting queues, while trees and tries broaden our insight into hierarchies and fast searches. Skip lists, motivated by coin flips, introduce controlled randomness with accuracy and precision.
