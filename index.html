What is the course about?

➖ Design and Analysis of Algorithms goes far beyond a branch of computer science — it forms the foundation of computational thinking. It plays a crucial role across science, engineering, business, and modern technology. The course helps us study natural patterns and processes that inspire efficient computational models. It strengthens analytical thinking, enabling us to approach complex problems with structured and optimised strategies.

What kind of data structures and algorithms have you studied?

➖ Exploring data structures feels like travelling through a fascinating system of ideas. Arrays and strings show continuity, linked lists mimic interconnected pages, stacks and queues mirror real-world behaviour like biscuit piles and waiting lines. Trees, tries, graphs, and skip lists expand our understanding of hierarchical, probabilistic, and efficient searching techniques. Each structure offers its own logic, purpose, and elegance — forming the backbone of problem solving.

How do you connect the course with real-time applications?

➖ Algorithms silently guide our everyday digital experiences — like the unseen air around us. Dijkstra’s Algorithm powers GPS navigation, sorting algorithms manage e-commerce data, and graph-based models fuel social media recommendations. They transform large scale problems into elegant, optimised solutions. These algorithms are the invisible intelligence behind convenience, speed, security, and personalisation in modern technology.
